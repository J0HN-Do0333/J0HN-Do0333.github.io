OUR COMMITTEES

    AFRICAN UNION

    Agenda I: The "Harmonious Horizons: Sino-African Alliance for shared growth"


    Insurgency has often been used in different contexts in the African Continent; colonial powers labeled any independence movements as a move of insurgency to maintain their control over the country. However, as time passed the way countries defined insurgency in Africa was based more around Islamic Jihadism. This was mostly due to events like the truck bombings of US Embassies in East Africa in 1998, and the 9/11 tragedy in 2001. Some reasons for the growing insurgency in this continent is due to nations having successive weak governments with rampant corruption, indemnity and disorganization..

    Poorly trained and unpaid military troops have begun mutinying or deserting their forces entirely. This non-professionalism of the soldiers, whose duties have been limited to protecting state leaders, have stained their reputation in the eyes of the public. Jihadist insurgents, however, are known to offer the public with fundamental services when the government fails to do so, convincing people to join their cause using it as a moral leverage. The African Union this year will be focusing on the ongoing Boko Haram insurgency in West Africa. Since the group’s founding, Boko Haram has wreaked havoc for more than a decade now. Launching attacks on government and UN buildings in demand for the creation of an Islamic State, the organization has not taken a look back and their methods, and crimes only seem to proliferate. The committee will be expected to address how to combat the massacres, suicide bombings, mass kidnappings conducted by the organization and putting a stop to their future activities entirely.
